# Traditional Business Plan (SBA Format)

## 1. Executive Summary

<!-- Write last. Summarize the entire plan in 1-2 pages. Cover: mission, product/service, target market, competitive advantage, financial highlights, and funding request. This is the first thing investors read — make it compelling. -->

## 2. Company Description

<!-- Describe your company: legal structure, history, mission statement, vision, and objectives. What does the business do? What market needs does it serve? What competitive advantages does it have? -->

## 3. Market Analysis

<!-- Demonstrate deep understanding of your industry and target market. Include: industry outlook, target market size and demographics, market trends, competitive landscape, regulatory environment. Reference PESTEL, Porter's Five Forces, and other analyses if completed. -->

## 4. Organization & Management

<!-- Describe organizational structure, ownership, management team bios, and board of advisors. Include an org chart if helpful. Highlight relevant experience and skills. -->

## 5. Products / Services Line

<!-- Describe what you sell or what service you offer. Cover: product/service description, lifecycle stage, intellectual property, R&D activities. What is the benefit to the customer? -->

## 6. Marketing & Sales Strategy

<!-- How will you attract and retain customers? Cover: market positioning, pricing strategy, sales process, distribution channels, advertising and promotion, customer retention strategy. -->

## 7. Funding Request

<!-- If seeking funding: how much do you need, how will you use it, what type of funding (equity, debt, convertible), and what terms are you offering? Include future funding requirements over the next 3-5 years. -->

## 8. Financial Projections (3-5 Year)

<!-- Provide: income statements, balance sheets, cash flow statements, capital expenditure budgets. Include assumptions behind projections. Reference the financial-projections template for detailed figures. -->

## 9. Appendix

<!-- Supporting documents: resumes, permits/licenses, leases, legal documents, contracts, letters of intent, market research data, reference letters. -->
